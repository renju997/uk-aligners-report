The teen acceleration

Adults still dominate revenue (73.59% in 2025), but the teen segment is now the fastest growing. Two specific signals:

  • BOS 2023 protocol update — clear aligners recognised as a valid first-line option for Class I cases in 12–17 year olds, conditional on >20 hours/day wear.
  • Align Technology Q3 2025 — teen and kids segment specifically called out as the volume growth driver in quarterly results.

Top patient motivations

From BOS and BDJ data, in approximate order of frequency:

  1. Crowding (most common)
  2. Aesthetic improvement / self-confidence — amplified by video-call self-perception
  3. Spacing
  4. Bite correction
  5. Pre-restorative preparation (implants, veneers, bonding) — the under-exploited cross-sell vector
💰
Cross-sell implication. Patients who present for aligner consultation often have other restorative needs that go unsurfaced. A structured smile journey (alignment → whitening → bonding → veneers → implants → retainers) typically raises lifetime patient value 2–3× vs. aligner-only delivery.

Section 3

Regulatory & Compliance Landscape

GDC, BOS and CQC have all reinforced that aligner treatment is regulated clinical activity — even when delivered remotely. The SmileDirectClub collapse is now the standard cautionary reference.

GDC — direct-to-consumer aligners

The General Dental Council's formal statement holds that:

  • Clear aligner treatment falls within the legal definition of dentistry, regardless of how it is initiated (including patient-taken impressions or remote scans).
  • Treatment must be provided by a GDC-registered dental professional.
  • "There is no effective substitute for a physical, clinical examination as the foundation for that assessment."
gdc-uk.org / standards-guidance / direct-to-consumer-orthodontics

BOS teledentistry guidance (Feb 2022)

  • All diagnostic and prescriptive decisions must be made by a treating orthodontist/dentist.
  • "The direct involvement of an appropriately trained and registered orthodontist/dentist is essential for the monitoring of orthodontic care."
  • Patients on aligner treatment must have direct contact with the responsible clinician and be able to arrange face-to-face appointments when needed.
bos.org.uk — Guidance on teledentistry and remote interactions in orthodontic care

SmileDirectClub — the cautionary tale

⚠️
SmileDirectClub announced bankruptcy on 8 December 2023 and shut all global operations on 10 December 2023. UK customers were left mid-treatment with no clinical handover and limited refund options (most relying on Section 75 credit-card protection). The event has reshaped UK consumer perception of remote-only aligner models and is now used directly by clinic-led providers as a trust-positioning argument.
Which? consumer reporting; Refinery29; multiple UK dental practice explainers.

CQC position

The Care Quality Commission's "Dental mythbuster 39: Direct-to-consumer orthodontics" reinforces that remote-led aligner services are regulated activity. CQC inspection scope extends to any aligner-providing practice in England — Treeline's CQC registration is itself a marketable trust asset.

cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/dentists/dental-mythbuster-39-direct-consumer-orthodontics
